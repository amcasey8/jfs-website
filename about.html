About the Project Lead

A Massachusetts-raised journalist-turned-geographer, Alex Casey (pictured) is a PhD student in the Department of Geography at the University of Oregon specializing in pyro- and economic geography. Her M.S. thesis explored the construction of hazard in Oregon's SB 762/80 maps. Alex holds a B.A. in Global Studies with a focus in Arabic and Sustainable Development from the University of California, Berkeley, where she graduated with high honors.

In addition to her research at the University of Oregon, Alex has collaborated with institutions such as the National Renewable Energy Laboratory and the Climate and Community Project. Alex's research spans topics from the socioeconomic impacts of ecotourism in Jordan to the implementation of U.S. climate adaptation policies.

Her current Joint Fire Science Project work focuses on the implementation of Oregon’s Senate Bill 762/80, and insurance issues throughout the Pacific Northwest.
